11. Referral system / Channel partner 30% commission
12. 1. The "Double-Sided" Win (Crucial)
A 30% commission is a fantastic incentive for the referrer, but successful programs (like Dropbox and Uber) almost always use double-sided rewards.

The Strategy: While the referrer gets 30% commission, the referred user needs a strong incentive to sign up through that specific link rather than going directly to your website.
Example: "Give a 20% discount on their first 3 months, Get 30% commission." This reduces the friction for the referrer—they don't feel like they are just selling; they feel like they are giving a friend a great deal.
2. Gamified Progression (The "Goal Gradient Effect")
People are more motivated to complete a task the closer they feel they are to the finish line.

Visual Milestones: Instead of just a dashboard that says "Total Referrals: 2", show a progress bar. "You are 1 referral away from unlocking Silver Tier!"
Tiered Commission Hooks: Start them at a base rate and let them "level up."
Level 1 (Partner): 30% Commission
Level 2 (Pro - 5 Referrals): 30% Commission + Free internal perk/credit
Level 3 (Elite - 20 Referrals): 40% Commission + Top Tier Badge
3. Immediate "Dopamine Hits" and Micro-Rewards
If someone refers a friend, they might have to wait a month for the friend to pay and the 30% commission to clear. That delay kills momentum.

Instant Gratification: Give them a small, immediate reward just for sending the invite or when the friend signs up for a free trial (e.g., unlocking a minor premium feature for a day, or giving them a "First Blood" badge on their profile).
Celebration: When a referral converts, trigger a visual celebration (confetti, a congratulatory pop-up) immediately upon their next login.
4. Leaderboards & Social Proof (Ego and Competition)
Humans are naturally competitive.

Public/Private Leaderboards: Create a "Top Partners of the Month" board. If users see others making significant commissions or climbing the ranks, it triggers FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) and competitive drive.
Status Indicators: Give top referrers a special badge next to their name in the app or community community.
5. Seamless "Frictionless" UX
The most addictive programs require zero thought to participate in.

One-Click Sharing: Embed share buttons (WhatsApp, Email, LinkedIn) directly in their daily workflow, not hidden beneath a "Settings > Advanced" menu.
Pre-Written Copy: Provide them with high-converting, pre-written messages that they don't have to think about. "Hey, I've been using this tool to automate my WhatsApp marketing. Here's a private link for 20% off..."
Contextual Nudges: Prompt them to refer exactly when they are happiest. Did they just successfully send a massive campaign? Boom: "Great job on your campaign! Know someone else who needs this? Share your link for a 30% cut."
6. Transparency & Trust
People get addicted when they know the system works and they aren't being scammed out of their leads.

Real-Time Tracking: Provide a flawless dashboard where they can see exactly what status their referrals are in (e.g., Link Clicked → Signed Up → Trial Active → Paid (Commission Earned!)). The anticipation of watching a lead move through the funnel is highly engaging.
Summary Recommendation
If you decide to move forward with the 30% Partner Program, start with Double-Sided Rewards (give the new user a discount so the link is easy to share) and a Real-Time Tracking Dashboard. Once that baseline is set, you can layer on the gamified elements like tiers, badges, and leaderboards to make it truly addictive.
